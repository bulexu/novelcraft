Chapter 14: The Whispering Wind

The cityscape of Neo-Beijing didn't just glow; it breathed. Beneath the thousand-watt hum of the commercial district, a softer, more ancient sound began to stir. It was a wind that shouldn't exist in a world controlled by atmospheric scrubbers—a dry, whistling draft that smelled of burnt ozone and cedarwood.

Sovereign adjusted the lapels of his charcoal coat, his eyes darting toward the alleyway where the shadow had first flickered. Beside him, Neon was already tapping at her wrist-comm, her fingers moving in a rhythmic dance that mirrored the flickering holographic displays.

"The encryption is shifting," Neon whispered, her voice barely audible over the distant roar of a maglev train. "It's not code anymore. It's... frequency. Someone is playing the city like a flute."

Sovereign didn't answer. He was listening to the wind. It spoke of things the data-stream couldn't capture: the forgotten stories of the underground, the silent revolutions being plotted in the gaps between the server towers. The Whispering Wind wasn't a phenomenon; it was a warning.
